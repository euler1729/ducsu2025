üìà Statistical Methodology & Model Framework

Bayesian Probability Framework

Uses prior historical data combined with current observational evidence to calculate posterior probabilities. Each scenario updates the base probabilities using Bayes' theorem, accounting for changing political conditions.

Monte Carlo Simulations

Runs 10,000 simulated elections under current conditions to calculate win probabilities. Each simulation randomly varies factors like turnout, undecided voter allocation, and campaign effectiveness within realistic ranges.

Multi-Factor Weighted Scoring

Combines organizational strength (30%), media visibility (20%), social movement credibility (25%), and hall influence (25%) with adjustments for internal cohesion and position-specific requirements.

Confidence Intervals & Uncertainty

Provides 90% confidence intervals around each probability estimate, acknowledging uncertainty from the large undecided voter population and first election since 2019 creating historical data limitations.

Probability vs. Vote Share 35% winning probability means winning 35 out of 100 identical elections, NOT receiving 35% of votes. A panel can win with 25% of votes in a 5-way race.
Confidence Intervals 90% confidence means we're 90% certain the true probability lies within the given range. Wider intervals indicate greater uncertainty from limited data.
Why Modest Probabilities With 5 competitive panels and 48.5% undecided voters, even favorites rarely exceed 45% probability. This reflects genuine electoral uncertainty.
